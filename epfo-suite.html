📐 EPS Pension Formula

Monthly Pension = (Pensionable Salary × Pensionable Service) ÷ 70

Pensionable Salary = Average of last 60 months' Basic + DA, capped at ₹15,000 (unless higher wage option exercised under 2014 amendment).
Minimum pension = ₹1,000/month guaranteed by government.
Maximum service counted = 35 years. If service ≥ 20 years, bonus of 2 years added.
Early pension (age 50–58): reduced by 4% for each year before 58. Deferred pension (after 58 up to 60): increased by 4% per year.

Permanent disability while in service, regardless of years served. No minimum service needed.

📋 General Rules for All Advances

  • Online claims via UAN portal settle in 3–7 working days; offline (through employer) takes 15–30 days.
  • UAN must be fully KYC-verified: Aadhaar + PAN + Bank account linked and employer-approved.
  • Employer must have digitally approved your KYC on the EPFO employer portal for online claims to work.
  • Medical advance has NO minimum service requirement — claimable from Day 1 of employment.
  • Marriage/Education advances: maximum 3 times in a lifetime combined.
  • Housing purchase advance: only once in lifetime from EPF.
  • Advance for unemployment: claim only after 2 months of leaving job (75% of corpus).
  • If claim is rejected, EPFO must give reason in writing. You can appeal to Regional PF Commissioner.
  • Advances do NOT need to be repaid — they are deducted from your final PF balance at settlement.
  • Amount credited directly to your linked bank account (must be same as KYC bank).

🔑 The Golden Rule

PF withdrawal is completely tax-free if you have completed 5 or more continuous years of service (including transfer to new employer without break). Below 5 years, the entire amount (employer share + interest) becomes taxable income in the year of withdrawal.

💡 Pro Strategy: Split Withdrawal Across Two Financial Years

If your PF amount is large (say ₹6 lakh), withdrawing all in one year pushes you into a higher tax slab.

Example: Withdraw ₹3L before March 31 (FY 2024-25) and ₹3L after April 1 (FY 2025-26). Each ₹3L is taxed separately, keeping you in a lower slab and potentially saving ₹15,000–30,000 in tax.

Remember: This only matters if service < 5 years. Above 5 years, the whole amount is tax-free regardless.

If your PF balance is wrong, claim is stuck, employer hasn't deposited, or money is missing — follow this escalation path step by step.

1

Verify via UAN Portal First

Login at unifiedportal-mem.epfindia.gov.in → View Passbook → Check all transactions. Download PDF. Verify: (a) employer is depositing monthly, (b) your KYC is complete, (c) UAN is activated.

2

Raise Grievance on EPFiGMS

Go to epfigms.gov.in → Register complaint → Select category (Claim Delay / Wrong Balance / Employer Not Depositing / Transfer Pending etc.) → Submit → Note your token/grievance number for tracking. Response usually within 15 working days.

3

Call EPFO Helpdesk

Toll-free: 1800-118-005 (Mon–Sat, 9 AM–6 PM). Email: grievance@epfindia.gov.in. Have ready: UAN, Establishment Code (from salary slip), nature of complaint, reference number from EPFiGMS.

4

Visit Regional PF Office (RPFC)

If no resolution in 30 days — visit your nearest EPFO Regional/Sub-Regional office. Find your office at epfindia.gov.in → Contact Us → Regional Offices. Meet Dealing Assistant or AAO (Assistant Accounts Officer). Carry all documents listed below.

5

Escalate to Central PF Commissioner (CPFC)

If RPFC doesn't resolve in 30 more days: write a formal letter to Central Provident Fund Commissioner, EPFO HQ, 14 Bhikaji Cama Place, New Delhi – 110066. Send by registered post with all complaint history attached.

6

Legal Recourse — Labour Court / High Court

Section 7A, EPF & MP Act 1952: File for adjudication of EPF disputes before the EPFO enforcement officer or Labour Court. For employer not depositing PF: file complaint under Section 14 (criminal liability — imprisonment up to 3 years + fine). Engage a labour law advocate.

If you have worked with multiple employers and accidentally got multiple UANs, you must merge/deactivate the duplicate. EPFO rules mandate one UAN per employee for life. Here is everything you need to know.

⚠️ Can Two UANs Be Merged?
❌ Technically "Merge" is NOT possible

EPFO does not merge two UANs in the sense that one disappears. What actually happens is: the old/duplicate UAN is deactivated and all PF balances linked to it are transferred to your active (correct) UAN via Form 13.

✅ What You Should Do

Keep your oldest or most-used UAN active. Raise a request to deactivate the duplicate UAN. Transfer all PF balances from the old UAN/PF accounts into your active UAN using Form 13.

📋 Step-by-Step: How to Deactivate Duplicate UAN
1

Identify Your Two UANs

Check your salary slips, Form 16, or ask HR of both companies. You can also check at unifiedportal-mem.epfindia.gov.in → Know Your UAN using your Aadhaar or PAN. Note down both UAN numbers and which employer they are linked to.

2

Decide Which UAN to Keep (Active)

Generally keep the older UAN as your primary active one — it has more service history. If one is linked to more PF balance, keep that. You cannot keep both active.

3

Report to Your Current Employer

Inform your present employer's HR in writing that you have two UANs. Give them both UAN numbers. The current employer is responsible for reporting duplicate UAN to EPFO on your behalf via their employer portal.

4

Employer Raises Deactivation Request on EPFO Portal

Employer logs into EPFO Employer Portal → Member → Mark Exit → Report Duplicate UAN. They submit both UANs and indicate which one to keep active. EPFO processes deactivation of the duplicate UAN.

5

Transfer PF Balance via Form 13

Once old UAN is deactivated, file Form 13 online via active UAN portal (Online Services → One Member One EPF Account) OR offline. This transfers all PF/EPS balance from the old PF account(s) into your active UAN account. Service period is also consolidated for EPS calculation.

6

Verify Consolidation

After 20–30 working days, check your active UAN passbook to confirm the transferred balance appears. Download the updated passbook. Your combined service years will now reflect correctly for EPS pension calculation.

🌐 Online Method (Self-Service)
  1. Login to unifiedportal-mem.epfindia.gov.in with your ACTIVE UAN
  2. Go to Online Services → One Member - One EPF Account
  3. Enter your old/duplicate UAN and old PF account number
  4. Select which employer (previous or current) will attest
  5. Submit → Employer approves digitally on their portal
  6. EPFO deactivates old UAN + transfers balance automatically
  7. Track at Track Claim Status on UAN portal
📄 Offline Method (Through Employer)
  1. Write a letter to your current employer's HR mentioning both UANs
  2. Employer submits duplicate UAN report to EPFO employer portal
  3. Download Form 13 from epfindia.gov.in → Downloads → Forms
  4. Fill Form 13 with old PF account details + active UAN details
  5. Employer signs + stamps Form 13
  6. Submit to EPFO Regional Office. Collect acknowledgment.
  7. Follow up after 30 days if no update received

⚠️ Important Warnings
  • !Having two active UANs is illegal under EPF rules. Do not ignore it — it can cause problems during final settlement and pension calculation.
  • !Never try to withdraw from both UANs — EPFO can flag your account and freeze both if dual UAN is detected.
  • !EPS pension service years will only be correctly calculated after both PF accounts are transferred to the single active UAN.
  • !If your employer has already deducted PF but linked it to the wrong/duplicate UAN — employer is responsible to fix this at their EPFO portal.
